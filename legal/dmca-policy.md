## 2. Filing a DMCA Notice of Infringement

If you believe in good faith that content on Appio infringes your copyright, you may submit a DMCA notice. Your notice must be in writing (email is acceptable) and must include **all** of the following elements as required by 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3):

1. **Your physical or electronic signature** (or that of a person authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner)

2. **Identification of the copyrighted work** claimed to have been infringed. If multiple works are covered by a single notice, a representative list of such works.

3. **Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing** and that is to be removed or access to which is to be disabled, and information reasonably sufficient to permit Appio to locate the material. Please include:
   - The URL where the infringing material is located (e.g., `https://[app-id].appiousercontent.com/...`)
   - The username or app name associated with the material
   - A description of the specific content claimed to infringe

4. **Your contact information**: name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.

5. **A statement** that you have a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

6. **A statement, made under penalty of perjury**, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

## 3. False Claims and Misrepresentations

**Important:** Under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f), any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing may be liable for damages, including costs and attorneys' fees, incurred by the alleged infringer or by Appio.

Do not submit a DMCA notice unless you are certain the material is infringing and that the use is not subject to fair use, license, or another exception. **If in doubt, consult an attorney.**

## 4. Counter-Notification

If you are a user of Appio and your content has been removed or disabled in response to a DMCA notice, you may submit a counter-notification if you believe the removal was a mistake or misidentification. A counter-notification must include all of the following, as required by 17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(3):

1. **Your physical or electronic signature.**

2. **Identification of the material** that has been removed or disabled and the location at which the material appeared before it was removed or disabled (provide the URL).

3. **A statement, made under penalty of perjury,** that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.

4. **Your name, mailing address, and telephone number.**

5. **A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which your address is located** (or, if your address is outside the United States, the judicial district in which Appio is located), and that you will accept service of process from the person who provided the original DMCA notice or an agent of that person.

## 5. Repeat Infringer Policy

In accordance with 17 U.S.C. § 512(i), Appio has adopted a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances and at our sole discretion, the accounts of users who are deemed repeat infringers.

**Definition of repeat infringer**: A user against whom we receive **three (3) valid DMCA notices** within a **rolling 12-month period** is considered a repeat infringer and will have their account terminated. We may also terminate accounts of users who we, in our sole judgment, believe are infringing intellectual property rights, even if no formal DMCA notice has been received.

We track DMCA notices received against each user and apply this policy consistently.

Termination under this policy is permanent. Affected users may not create new accounts. Attempts to evade termination by creating new accounts are a separate violation of the [Acceptable Use Policy](acceptable-use-policy.md).
