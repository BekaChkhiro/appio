## 1. What Are Cookies and Storage Technologies?

**Cookies** are small text files that websites place on your device. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to site operators.

**Local storage**, **session storage**, and **IndexedDB** are browser APIs that allow websites to store data on your device. They are not technically "cookies" but serve similar functions and are subject to similar legal rules in many jurisdictions.

**Service workers** are scripts that run in the background of your browser to enable offline functionality, push notifications, and caching for Progressive Web Applications.

## 2. Categories of Storage Technologies We Use

We classify Storage Technologies into the following categories. Where consent is required (EU/UK/Switzerland and equivalent jurisdictions), we will request it before non-essential categories are activated.

### 2.1 Strictly Necessary

These are essential for the Service to function. They cannot be disabled without breaking core functionality. We do **not** require consent for strictly necessary technologies under most laws.

| Name | Type | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| `appio_session` | Cookie | Maintains your authenticated session on the Website (token-based) | Session |
| `appio_csrf` | Cookie | Cross-Site Request Forgery protection | Session |
| `firebase_*` | Local storage | Firebase Authentication state | Persistent until logout |
| `consent_state` | Local storage | Records your cookie consent choices | 12 months |
| `app_locale` | Local storage | Your selected language preference | Persistent |

### 2.2 Functional

These remember your preferences to improve your experience. We treat these as **opt-in** in jurisdictions that require consent.

| Name | Type | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| `theme_preference` | Local storage | Light/dark mode preference | Persistent |
| `recent_prompts` | Local storage | Recent prompts for autocomplete | 30 days |
| `tutorial_seen` | Local storage | Tracks whether you have completed onboarding | Persistent |

### 2.3 Analytics

These help us understand how users interact with the Service so we can improve it. **Opt-in** in EU/UK/Switzerland.

| Provider | Purpose | Data Collected | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PostHog** | Product analytics, funnel analysis, feature flags | Anonymized event data, device info, IP address (truncated) | Up to 7 years (configurable) |
| **Sentry** | Error and performance monitoring | Error context, stack traces, breadcrumbs (no PII unless intentionally captured) | 90 days |

We do **not** use third-party advertising cookies. We do **not** participate in cross-context behavioral advertising.

### 2.4 Generated Apps

**Generated Apps published by users may use their own Storage Technologies** to store user data on the device. Examples include:

- localStorage to store to-do items, expenses, notes, etc.
- IndexedDB for larger structured data
- A service worker (`sw.js`) for offline functionality and caching

**Storage in Generated Apps is controlled by the publishing user, not by Appio.** When you install or use a Generated App, the Storage Technologies it uses are governed by the privacy practices of that app's publisher, not by this Policy. We do not access local storage in Generated Apps.

Generated Apps served from `*.appiousercontent.com` are sandboxed: they cannot set cookies for `appio.app`, and they do not have access to your Appio account session.

## 3. Consent and Your Choices

### 3.1 EU / UK / Switzerland (ePrivacy)

When you first visit the Website from these jurisdictions, you will see a consent banner with the following options:

- **Accept all** — accept all categories of Storage Technologies
- **Reject all** — accept only strictly necessary technologies
- **Manage preferences** — choose individual categories

You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Preferences" link in the Website footer or in the Mobile App settings. Withdrawal of consent is as easy as giving it and does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

### 3.2 Other Jurisdictions

Where consent is not legally required, we apply non-essential Storage Technologies based on legitimate interests, but you may still opt out at any time via the same controls.

### 3.3 Browser Controls

You can also control Storage Technologies through your browser settings:

- **Block all cookies** — may break the Service
- **Block third-party cookies**
- **Clear cookies and storage**
- **Use private/incognito mode**

Useful links:

- Chrome: https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647
- Safari: https://support.apple.com/guide/safari/manage-cookies-sfri11471
- Firefox: https://support.mozilla.org/kb/cookies-information-websites-store-on-your-computer
- Edge: https://support.microsoft.com/microsoft-edge

You may also use the **Global Privacy Control** (GPC) signal. We honor GPC signals from browsers that support it.

### 3.4 Mobile App

The Mobile App does not use HTTP cookies but uses similar local storage technologies (AsyncStorage, MMKV, etc.) for the same categories described above. You can manage analytics consent in the in-app Privacy settings.

### 3.5 Do Not Track

Browsers may send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. There is no industry consensus on how DNT should be honored. We treat DNT signals as a request to opt out of analytics.
